# PDM Information / Resources

## About the Parish Development Model (PDM)

The **Parish Development Model (PDM)** is a Government of Uganda national development strategy aimed at transitioning subsistence households to the money economy. It was rolled out in FY 2021/22 following recommendations from the National Development Plan (NDP I & II) mid-term assessments.

> *"The PDM is the last mile strategy for service delivery and improvement of incomes and welfare of all Ugandans at the household level."* — PDM Implementation Guidelines

### What is the PDM?

The PDM is a multi‑sectoral strategy to improve incomes and welfare of Ugandans by bringing services closer to the citizens. It makes the Parish the lowest administrative epicentre for grassroots planning, budgeting, reporting and service delivery by both state and non‑state actors.

### What Does the PDM Aim to Achieve?

**The main aim** of the Parish Development Model is to increase household incomes and improve the quality of life of Ugandans, with a specific focus on the **total economic transformation of households** by moving them out of the subsistence economy into the money economy **within 5 years**.

#### Economic Transformation Targets:
- Every household having **minimum income** to access basic human needs (food, shelter, clothing, health, education, security, spirituality)
- Every household having **assets** necessary to generate adequate income and savings
- Every household earning a **minimum of UGX 20 million per annum** by 2025

#### Key Statistics:
- **3.5 million** households targeted (subsistence farming households)
- **39.2%** of households in Uganda are at subsistence level
- **10,594** parishes across the country

> *"The objective is to move the 3.5 million households that are still subsistence farming to the money economy."* — H.E. President Yoweri Kaguta Museveni

## The 7 Pillars of the PDM

The PDM is implemented along **seven pillars**:

| Pillar | Focus Area | Lead Ministry/Agency |
|--------|------------|---------------------|
| **Pillar 1** | **Production, Processing & Marketing** — Enhancing agricultural value chains, productivity and market access | MAAIF |
| **Pillar 2** | **Infrastructure & Economic Services** — Roads, electricity, water, markets, telecommunications | MoWT |
| **Pillar 3** | **Financial Inclusion** — Parish Revolving Fund (PRF), savings, credit, insurance, business development services | MoFPED |
| **Pillar 4** | **Social Services** — Health, education, and social protection | MoES, MoH, MoGLSD |
| **Pillar 5** | **Mindset Change** — Community mobilisation and attitude transformation | MoGLSD |
| **Pillar 6** | **Parish-Based Management Information System** — Data collection, digitisation, and citizen information systems | MICT |
| **Pillar 7** | **Governance & Administration** — Recruitment of parish chiefs, establishment of PDCs and working groups | MoLG |

> *"The PDM underlying spirit is the harmonisation of Government interventions with every MDA focusing on delivering services closer to the people and measurable results at the Parish level."*

## Implementation Structure

### Parish-Level Structure
The PDM implementation structure at the parish level includes:

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Parish Chief / Town Agent** | Accounting officer at parish level, coordinates all Government efforts at the parish |
| **Parish Development Committee (PDC)** | Provides oversight of PDM implementation, mobilises communities, oversees enterprise groups and SACCOs |
| **PDM SACCO** | Channels revolving funds to enterprise groups, manages loans and savings |
| **Enterprise Groups** | Groups of 10–30 household members engaged in common income-generating agro-based activities |
| **LC II Chairperson** | Chairs the PDC and leads community mobilisation |

### Local Government Structure
District and sub-county structures support parish-level implementation through:
- Technical guidance and oversight
- Capacity building and training
- Monitoring, evaluation, and reporting
- Coordination with MDAs and development partners

## Enterprises Eligible for Support

The PDM supports enterprises across the agricultural value chain:

### Value Chain Nodes
1. **Input Production & Supply** — Animal feed, seed, fertilisers, agro-chemicals, machinery, irrigation equipment
2. **Primary Production** — Crops, livestock, fish
3. **Handling, Processing & Storage** — Cleaning, drying, grading, storage, processing, value addition, packing
4. **Marketing, Trade & Distribution** — Wholesale, retail, exports

### Priority Commodities (18)
Coffee, Cotton, Cocoa, Cassava, Tea, Vegetable Oils (including Oil Palm), Maize, Rice, Sugar Cane, Fish, Dairy, Beef, Bananas, Beans, Avocado, Shea Nut, Cashew Nuts, Macadamia

> *"Each Parish shall choose projects that benefit all interested subsistence households. The flagship projects are identified and selected by the parish residents in a participatory manner."* — PDM Implementation Guidelines

## What is Provided Under the PDM?

Under the Parish Development Model, eligible households and groups receive:

- **Access to affordable credit** through the Parish Revolving Fund (PRF) at 6% annual interest
- **Business development services** and financial literacy training
- **Agricultural extension services** and technical advice
- **Support for enterprise selection and group formation**
- **Links to value addition and market access**
- **Insurance protection** through the Uganda Agriculture Insurance Scheme (UAIS)

### UAIS Premium Subsidies:
| Farmer Category | Subsidy |
|-----------------|---------|
| Large-scale farmers | 30% |
| Small-scale farmers | 50% |
| Disaster-prone areas | 80% |
